# IST house-style notes (from recent IST papers, 2023–2026)

Distilled from full reads of 5 recent IST empirical/measurement papers + the Guide
for Authors. Our manuscript was aligned to these; keep them for future revisions.

## Mandatory / structural
- **Structured abstract** (IST will not handle a paper without one): five parts, in
  order, labels inline + emphasized. Singular period-terminated form is standard
  (*Context. Objective. Method. Results. Conclusion.*) — matches StartFlow, IST 197
  (2026). Ours already uses this.
- **Section skeleton:** Introduction → (Background/Related Work) → Methodology /
  Study Design → Results → Discussion → **Threats to Validity** (own top-level
  section, after Discussion) → Conclusion (and Future Work). "Study Design" is
  idiomatic.
- **Research paper ≤ 15,000 words** (tables/figures count ~200 words each).
- elsarticle + elsarticle-num; CRediT + competing-interest + data-availability +
  funding; highlights 3–5 bullets ≤85 chars.

## Language / register
- First-person **"we"**; **past tense for the procedure executed**, present tense
  for what the data show. (e.g. "we conducted…", "our findings show…")
- RQs labeled `RQ1…`, usually with a short tag: *"RQ1 (effectiveness): How…"*;
  Results organized one subsection per RQ. (Ours now tags them.)
- **Neutral, factual tone toward prior/related work** — say a study "reports a
  single metric" or "covers three ORMs", NOT "untrustworthy" / "marketing" /
  "flatters its product". This matters doubly for a paper claiming neutrality.
- Assertive when statistically backed; hedge generalization ("may not generalize…");
  recommendations with "should".
- American spelling dominant (British accepted) — **no mixing within one paper**.
  Cross-refs: "Table 1 shows…", "Figure 5", "Section 3.1".
