\begin{abstract}
Node.js/Express applications reach relational databases through a spectrum of
access layers: native drivers, query builders, and object-relational mappers
(ORMs). Practitioners choose between them largely on the basis of vendor
benchmarks, which are fragmented, PostgreSQL-only, and report either throughput or
latency but rarely both. We present a neutral, reproducible benchmark of the full
access-layer taxonomy---native drivers (\texttt{pg}, \texttt{mysql2}), a query
builder (Knex), and six ORMs (Drizzle, Prisma, Sequelize, TypeORM, Objection,
MikroORM)---served through an identical Express application over five access
patterns, on both PostgreSQL and MySQL, reporting throughput and tail latency
(p50/p90/p99) together. Abstraction cost concentrates in relation-materializing
patterns: the deep/nested fetch spreads up to $6.4\times$ between the native driver
and the heaviest data-mapper ORM, while point reads and raw-SQL aggregation are
near layer-agnostic; the ordering is stable across engines and writes are
engine-bound.
\end{abstract}
