\begin{abstract}
Node.js/Express applications reach relational databases through a spectrum of
access layers: native drivers, query builders, and object-relational mappers
(ORMs). Practitioners choose between them largely on the basis of vendor
benchmarks, which are fragmented, PostgreSQL-only, and report throughput or
latency but rarely the tail (p99). We present a neutral, reproducible benchmark of
the full access-layer taxonomy (native drivers \texttt{pg} and \texttt{mysql2}, a
query builder Knex, and six ORMs: Drizzle, Prisma, Sequelize, TypeORM, Objection,
and MikroORM), served through an identical Express application over five access
patterns, on both PostgreSQL and MySQL, reporting throughput and tail latency
(p99) together. Abstraction cost concentrates in relation-materializing
patterns: the deep/nested fetch spreads up to $6.3\times$ between the native driver
and the heaviest data-mapper ORM, while raw-SQL aggregation is much less sensitive
and point reads only mildly affected; a same-plan control attributes most of that
spread to default eager-loading strategy and hydration rather than to the layers'
raw execution paths (within $1.4\times$); the coarse ordering is stable across engines,
and single-row inserts are engine-bound on MySQL under relaxed durability but
layer-dependent on PostgreSQL.
\end{abstract}
