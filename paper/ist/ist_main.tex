\title{A Comparative Analysis of Relational Database Access-Layer
  Performance in Express.js across PostgreSQL and MySQL}

\author[ug]{Mateusz Miotk}
\ead{mateusz.miotk@ug.edu.pl}
\affiliation[ug]{organization={Faculty of Mathematics, Physics and Informatics,
    University of Gda\'nsk},
  addressline={Wita Stwosza 57},
  postcode={80-308},
  city={Gda\'nsk},
  country={Poland}}

\begin{abstract}
\textbf{Context.} Node.js/Express services reach relational databases through a
spectrum of access layers---native drivers, query builders, and object-relational
mappers (ORMs)---yet practitioners choose between them largely on the basis of
vendor benchmarks that are fragmented, PostgreSQL-only, and report either
throughput or latency but seldom both.
\textbf{Objective.} We quantify, neutrally and reproducibly, the performance cost
of the access layer as a function of the layer, the database engine, and the
access pattern.
\textbf{Method.} An identical Express application is served through nine access
layers (the native drivers \texttt{pg} and \texttt{mysql2}, the query builder
Knex, and the ORMs Drizzle, Prisma, Sequelize, TypeORM, Objection and MikroORM)
over five access patterns, against PostgreSQL and MySQL, driven by an HTTP load
generator. We report throughput and tail latency (p50/p90/p99) jointly, hold the
connection pool constant, and verify that every layer returns identical results
through an automated cross-check.
\textbf{Results.} Abstraction cost concentrates in relation-materialising
patterns: the deep/nested fetch spreads up to $6.4\times$ between the native
driver and the heaviest data-mapper ORM, whereas point reads and raw-SQL
aggregation are near layer-agnostic. The relative ordering of layers is stable
across engines, while insert throughput is governed by the engine (MySQL's InnoDB
commit path) rather than the layer.
\textbf{Conclusion.} The choice of access layer matters most where the layer must
assemble a nested object graph; a reproducible harness and a checklist of
benchmarking pitfalls specific to this genre are released as a replication package.
\end{abstract}
