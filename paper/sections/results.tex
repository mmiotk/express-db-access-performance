\paragraph{RQ1 (overhead vs native baseline).}
On PostgreSQL the native \texttt{pg} driver leads most patterns, but the gap to the
best ORM is pattern-dependent: for the deep/nested fetch, Prisma (4{,}116~req/s) is
within a few percent of \texttt{pg} (4{,}173), whereas heavier data-mapper ORMs pay
a large penalty (MikroORM 686, Objection 936). Preliminary observation, to be
quantified with effect sizes.

\paragraph{RQ2 (engine dependence of the ranking).}
The ranking is \emph{not} engine-invariant. The range-scan pattern collapses on
MySQL for \emph{every} layer, including the native driver (\texttt{mysql2}
748~req/s vs \texttt{pg} 3{,}501), pointing to an engine/workload interaction
(large-offset ordering over low-cardinality timestamps) rather than an access-layer
effect---a confound we address in the workload redesign (Section~\ref{sec:threats}).

\paragraph{RQ3 (which patterns matter most).}
Layer choice matters least for point reads and inserts and most for the
deep/nested fetch and aggregation, where query strategy and row hydration dominate.
